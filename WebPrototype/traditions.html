THE TRADITIONS OF MUAY THAI

As a Nak Muay (student of Muay Thai), learning the background of the Thai boxing culture is important to truly understand what the martial art is all about. Besides understanding the ceremonial dance known as the wai kru ram muay, pre-fight rituals, and traditions that make this sport so unique.







MUAY THAI TRADITIONS

Just some of the many aspects of the different traditions in Muay Thai

  • The traditional dance that you see a majority of Muay thai fighters perform before the fight is known as the kru ram muay.

  • Thai boxing music, known as the Sarama, you can listen to and download that on the Master Muay Thai music page.

  • Thais are very superstitious people, so naturally the sport that they created will have plenty of superstitions surrounding it. Another one of these Thai boxing traditions come in the form of a mongkol (or mongkon) which is a Muay Thai headdress that every fighter wears.

  • Sak yant tattoos are done by buddhist monks with bamboo needles and supposedly give you good luck, protection from evil spirits, success in the ring and numerous other spiritual powers.
